# 	'Del' or 'Nabla' operator $\nabla$
the del operator is defined as the following *vector*:
$$
\vec\nabla = \dfrac{\partial}{\partial x} \vec{a_x} + \dfrac{\partial}{\partial y} \vec{a_y} + \dfrac{\partial}{\partial z} \vec{a_z}
$$
$\nabla$ is treated in every way as an ordinary vector with the the difference that *partial derivatives* result instead of *products of scalars*.
which will be more apparent in the next topic
## divergence 
a *dot operation* takes a vector and returns a scalar
$$
\nabla \cdot \vec{v} = \dfrac{\partial v_x}{\partial x} + \dfrac{\partial v_y}{\partial y} + \dfrac{\partial v_z}{\partial z}
$$
notice the result isn't the sum of each vector component multiplied, rather the partial derivative of each component of $\vec{v}$
## gradient
takes a scalar and returns a vector
$$
\nabla f = \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}\vec{a_x} + \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}\vec{a_y} + \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial z}\vec{a_z}
$$
**Note** the difference between a *scalar* and a *constant*
- constant: a *fixed value* that doesn't change, a number
- a scalar: something that is *NOT a vector*. it CAN be a constant of a variable or a function as long as can be described by just one value
## curl
takes a vector and returns another vector
$$
\nabla \times \vec{v} =
\begin{bmatrix}
	\vec{a_x} & \vec{a_y} & \vec{a_z} \\
	\partial/\partial x & \partial/\partial y & \partial/\partial z\\
	\vec{v_x} & \vec{v_y} & \vec{v_z}\\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
# divergence theorem
from: 
1. $\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{D}  = \dfrac{Q_{\text{en}}}{\Delta V} =\large\rho_v$    (**divergence equation**)
2. $\oint_\text{surface} \vec D \cdot \vec{dS} = \iiint \rho_VdV$

we get: 
$$
\oint_\text{surface} \vec D \cdot \vec{dS} = \iiint \left(\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{D}\right)\,dV
$$
## divergence equation for each coordinate system:

### Cartesian
$$
\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{D} = \frac{\partial D_x}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial D_y}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial D_z}{\partial z}
$$
### Cylindrical
$$
\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{D} = \frac{1}{\rho} \frac{\partial}{\partial \rho} (\rho D_\rho) + \frac{1}{\rho} \frac{\partial D_\phi}{\partial \phi} + \frac{\partial D_z}{\partial z}
$$
### Spherical
$$
\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{D} = \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial}{\partial r} (r^2 D_r) + \frac{1}{r \sin\theta} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} (\sin\theta D_\theta) + \frac{1}{r \sin\theta} \frac{\partial D_\phi}{\partial \phi}
$$
## proof for the divergence equation
### assume a very small cube with dimensions $\Delta x ,\Delta y ,\Delta z$ and volume: $\Delta V$ and point $P$ at its center with flux density


$$
\begin{array}{l}
	\vec D_p = D_{x_o}\vec a_x + D_{y_o}\vec a_y + D_{z_o}\vec a_z \qquad [c/m^2] \\\\
	\because \oint_\text{surface} \vec D \cdot \vec{ds} = q_{en} \\
	\iint_\text{front} + \iint_\text{back} + \iint_\text{right} + \iint_\text{left} + \iint_\text{top} +\iint_\text{bottom}  = q_{en}\\\\
	
	\iint_\text{front} \vec D \cdot \vec{\text{ds}} = D_{x\text{ front}} \Delta y \Delta z\\
	\text{where: } \vec D_{x_\text{front}} =  D_{x_o} + \dfrac{\Delta x}{2} \dfrac{\partial D_x}{\partial x}\\
	\iint_\text{front} \vec D \cdot \vec{\text{ds}} = \left( D_{x_o} + \dfrac{\Delta x}{2}\dfrac{\partial D_x}{\partial x} \right) \Delta y \Delta z \qquad (i) \\\\
	
	\iint_\text{back} \vec D \cdot \vec{\text{ds}} = -D_{x\text{ back}} \Delta y \Delta z\\
	\text{where: } \vec D_{x_\text{back}} =  D_{x_o} - \dfrac{\Delta x}{2} \dfrac{\partial D_x}{\partial x}\\
	\iint_\text{back} \vec D \cdot \vec{\text{ds}} = \left(- D_{x_o} + \dfrac{\Delta x}{2}\dfrac{\partial D_x}{\partial x} \right) \Delta y \Delta z \qquad (ii) \\\\
\end{array}
$$

$$
\begin{array}{l}
	\color{#acffcc}\text{from (i) \& (ii)}\\
	\iint_\text{front} + \iint_\text{back} = \dfrac{\partial D_x}{dx} \Delta x \Delta y \Delta z \qquad(1) \\
	\color{#acffcc}\text{applying the same steps for each face: }\\
	\iint_\text{right} + \iint_\text{left} = \dfrac{\partial D_y}{dy} \Delta x \Delta y \Delta z \qquad(2)\\
	\iint_\text{top} + \iint_\text{bottom} = \dfrac{\partial D_z}{dz} \Delta x \Delta y \Delta z \qquad(3)\\\\
	\oint_\text{surface} \vec D \cdot \vec{ds} = (1) + (2) + (3) = Q_\text{en}\\
	\left( \dfrac{\partial D_x}{dx} +  \dfrac{\partial D_y}{dy} +  \dfrac{\partial D_z}{dz} \right)\Delta V = Q_\text{en}\\\\
	\vec\nabla \cdot \vec D = \dfrac{Q_\text{en}}{\Delta V} \\
	\Rightarrow \Large\color{#acffcc}\vec\nabla \cdot \vec D = \rho_V \\
	\Rightarrow \Large\color{#acffcc}\text{"divergence equation"}
\end{array}
$$
