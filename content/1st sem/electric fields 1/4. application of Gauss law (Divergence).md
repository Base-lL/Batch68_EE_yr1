## divergence theorem
from: 
1. $\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{D}  = \dfrac{Q_{\text{en}}}{\Delta V} =\large\rho_v$    (**divergence equation**)
2. $\oint_\text{surface} \vec D \cdot \vec{dS} = \iiint \rho_VdV$

we get: 
$$
\oint_\text{surface} \vec D \cdot \vec{dS} = \iiint \left(\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{D}\right)\,dV
$$
### divergence equation for each coordinate system:
$$
\large\begin{align*} 
\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{D} &= \frac{\partial D_x}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial D_y}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial D_z}{\partial z} && (\text{Cartesian}) \\[10pt]
&= \frac{1}{\rho} \frac{\partial}{\partial \rho} (\rho D_\rho) + \frac{1}{\rho} \frac{\partial D_\phi}{\partial \phi} + \frac{\partial D_z}{\partial z} && (\text{Cylindrical}) \\[10pt]
&= \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial}{\partial r} (r^2 D_r) + \frac{1}{r \sin\theta} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} (\sin\theta D_\theta) + \frac{1}{r \sin\theta} \frac{\partial D_\phi}{\partial \phi} && (\text{Spherical})
\end{align*}
$$
## proof for the divergence equation

$$
\begin{array}{l}
	\Large\color{#acffcc}\text{assume a very small cube with dimenstions: }\\
	\Large\Delta x ,\Delta y ,\Delta z, \quad\color{#acffcc}\text{and volume: } \color{#f7ecc2} \Delta V\\
	\Large\color{#acffcc}\text{and point "P" at its center with  flux density:}\\\\
	\vec D_p = D_{x_o}\vec a_x + D_{y_o}\vec a_y + D_{z_o}\vec a_z \qquad [c/m^2] \\\\
	\because \oint_\text{surface} \vec D \cdot \vec{ds} = q_{en} \\
	\iint_\text{front} + \iint_\text{back} + \iint_\text{right} + \iint_\text{left} + \iint_\text{top} +\iint_\text{bottom}  = q_{en}\\\\
	
	\iint_\text{front} \vec D \cdot \vec{\text{ds}} = D_{x\text{ front}} \Delta y \Delta z\\
	\text{where: } \vec D_{x_\text{front}} =  D_{x_o} + \dfrac{\Delta x}{2} \dfrac{\partial D_x}{\partial x}\\
	\iint_\text{front} \vec D \cdot \vec{\text{ds}} = \left( D_{x_o} + \dfrac{\Delta x}{2}\dfrac{\partial D_x}{\partial x} \right) \Delta y \Delta z \qquad (i) \\\\
	
	\iint_\text{back} \vec D \cdot \vec{\text{ds}} = -D_{x\text{ back}} \Delta y \Delta z\\
	\text{where: } \vec D_{x_\text{back}} =  D_{x_o} - \dfrac{\Delta x}{2} \dfrac{\partial D_x}{\partial x}\\
	\iint_\text{back} \vec D \cdot \vec{\text{ds}} = \left(- D_{x_o} + \dfrac{\Delta x}{2}\dfrac{\partial D_x}{\partial x} \right) \Delta y \Delta z \qquad (ii) \\\\
\end{array}
$$

$$
\begin{array}{l}
	\color{#acffcc}\text{from (i) \& (ii)}\\
	\iint_\text{front} + \iint_\text{back} = \dfrac{\partial D_x}{dx} \Delta x \Delta y \Delta z \qquad(1) \\
	\color{#acffcc}\text{applying the same steps for each face: }\\
	\iint_\text{right} + \iint_\text{left} = \dfrac{\partial D_y}{dy} \Delta x \Delta y \Delta z \qquad(2)\\
	\iint_\text{top} + \iint_\text{bottom} = \dfrac{\partial D_z}{dz} \Delta x \Delta y \Delta z \qquad(3)\\\\
	\oint_\text{surface} \vec D \cdot \vec{ds} = (1) + (2) + (3) = Q_\text{en}\\
	\left( \dfrac{\partial D_x}{dx} +  \dfrac{\partial D_y}{dy} +  \dfrac{\partial D_z}{dz} \right)\Delta V = Q_\text{en}\\\\
	\vec\nabla \cdot \vec D = \dfrac{Q_\text{en}}{\Delta V} \\
	\Rightarrow \Large\color{#acffcc}\vec\nabla \cdot \vec D = \rho_V \\
	\Rightarrow \Large\color{#acffcc}\text{"divergence equation"}
\end{array}
$$
