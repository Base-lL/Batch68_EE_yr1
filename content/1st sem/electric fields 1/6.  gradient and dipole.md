# calculate the electric field using gradient process for : $\vec E = -\vec\nabla V$ 
$$
\large\begin{align} 
\vec\nabla  \vec{V} &= \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} \vec{a_x} +\frac{\partial V}{\partial y} \vec{a_y} +\frac{\partial V}{\partial z} \vec{a_z}  & (\text{cartesian}) \\[10pt]
&= \frac{\partial V}{\partial \rho} \vec{a_\rho} + \frac{1}{\rho}  \frac{\partial V}{\partial \phi} \vec{a_\phi} + \frac{\partial V}{\partial z} \vec{a_z} & (\text{Cylindrical}) \\[10pt]
&= \frac{\partial V}{\partial r} \vec{a_r}+ \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial V}{\partial \theta} \vec{a_\theta}+ \frac{1}{r \sin\theta} \frac{\partial V}{\partial \phi} \vec{a_\phi} & (\text{Spherical})
\end{align}
$$
## note that $\vec\nabla V$ and $\vec\nabla \cdot V$ are *NOT THE SAME* , refer back to the divergence section
# Dipole
## for 2 point charges that are
- equal in magnitude $Q$
- opposite in sign
- separated by a -very- small distance $d$
## the potential difference at a point $P(R,\theta,\phi)$ is: 
$$
\large\begin{align}
V &= V_{+Q} + V_{-Q} \\[3px]
&= \dfrac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0\, R_1} + \dfrac{-Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0\, R_2} \\[3px]
&= \dfrac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\left(\dfrac{1}{R_1} - \dfrac{1}{R_2} \right)\\[3px]
&= \dfrac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\left(\dfrac{R_2 - R_1}{R_1 R_2} \right)\\[3px]
\end{align}
$$
## where $R_1\;,\;R_2$ are the distance between P and each point
$$
\large\begin{array}{l}
	\Large\color{#acffcc}\text{since: } \\[2.5px]
		R \gg d \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_2 - R_1  d\cos\theta\\	R_1 \simeq R_2 \simeq R \quad\Rightarrow\quad R_1 R_2 \simeq R^2 \\[2.5px]
	\Large\color{#acffcc}\text{then: }\\[5px]
	 V=\dfrac{Q\,d\cos\theta}{4\pi\epsilon_0\,R^2}
\end{array}
$$
## the dipole moment: $\vec P = Q \vec d \quad [c\cdot m]$ 
$$
\large V=\dfrac{\vec P \cdot \vec{a_R} }{4\pi\epsilon_0\,R^2} + \dfrac{\vec P \cdot \vec{R} }{4\pi\epsilon_0\,R^3}
$$
## the electric field due to a dipole with center at the origin
$$
\large\vec E = \dfrac{ |P| }{4\pi\epsilon_0\,R^3} \left[ 2\cos\theta\;\vec{a_R} + \sin \theta\;\vec{a_\theta} \right]
$$
