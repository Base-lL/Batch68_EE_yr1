# Cartesian coordinates ($x,y,z$):
$$
\large p=(x,\;y,\;z)\quad \text{ where: } -\infty<x,\;y,\;z<\infty
$$

## displacement vector:
- **of a point**: it's the difference between the point and the origin point $O = (0,\,0,\,0)$
- for a vector $\vec{R_{12}}$ *coming from* $P_1$ and *ends in* $P_2$ : $\vec{R_{12}} = \vec{P_{2}} - \vec{P_{1}}$ 
- $\vec{R_{12}} = -\vec{R_{21}}$
## integration element:
![[Pasted image 20251027182152.png]]

- **length element:** $\vec{dL} = dx\,\vec{a_x}\;+ dy\,\vec{a_y}\;+ dz\,\vec{a_z}$
- **surface element:** $\vec{dS} = dy\,dz\,\vec{a_x}\;+ dx\,dz\,\vec{a_y}\;+ dx\,dy\,\vec{a_z}$
	- any surface is described using *only one axis*
	- the direction of any surface is its normal unit vector $\vec{a_n}$
- **volume element:** $dV = dx\,dy\,dz$
## vectors (assumed a vector $\vec{A}$ ):
$$
\vec{A} = A_x\,\vec{a_x}\;+ A_y\,\vec{a_y}\;+ A_z\,\vec{a_z} \quad \text{, where: }\qquad \vec{a_x}\,,\;\vec{a_y}\,,\;\vec{a_y}\quad \text{are unit vectors} \qquad,\qquad A_x\,,\,A_x\,,\,A_x\,\text{ are scalar components}
$$
$$
|\vec{A}| = \sqrt{{ A_x}^2 + {A_y}^2 + {A_z}^2} \qquad  \qquad\vec{a_A} = \dfrac{\vec{A}}{|A|}\;=\;\dfrac{\vec{A_x}}{|A|}\,\vec{a_x}\,+\,\dfrac{\vec{A_y}}{|A|}\,\vec{a_y}\,+\,\dfrac{\vec{A_z}}{|A|}\,\vec{a_z}
$$
$$
\Large\begin{array}\\
\vec{a_x}\;\times\;\vec{a_y}\;=\;\vec{a_z}\\
\vec{a_y}\;\times\;\vec{a_z}\;=\;\vec{a_x}\\
\vec{a_z}\;\times\;\vec{a_x}\;=\;\vec{a_y}
\end{array}
$$
## dot product:
$$\large\begin{align}
\vec{A}\;\cdot\vec{B}\quad&= A_x\,B_x\;+\;A_y\,B_y\;+\;A_z\,B_z \\
&=|A|\,|B|\cos\theta_{AB}
\end{align}$$
**the dot product of 2 vectors:** 
- is a scalar product
- if the vectors are *parallel and equal* = 1
- if the vectors are *perpendicular* = 0
## cross product:
$$
\large\vec{A}\;\times\vec{B}\quad=\quad
\begin{vmatrix}
	\vec{a_x} & \vec{a_y} & \vec{a_z} \\
	A_x & A_y & A_z \\
	B_x & B_y & B_z \\
\end{vmatrix}\quad
=|A|\,|B|\sin\theta_{AB}
$$
**the cross product of 2 vectors:** 
- is a vector that's perpendicular to them
- if the vectors are *parallel* = 0
- if the vectors are *perpendicular* = 1
# Cylindrical coordinates ($\rho,\phi,z$) :
![[Pasted image 20251118203440.png]]
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\rho : \text{starts from origin and increase over x-axis} & 0\le\rho<\infty \\
\phi : \text{angle that starts from the x-axis and moves counter-clockwise to the y-axis} \qquad& 0 \le \phi < 2\pi \\
z : \text{like in cartesian coordinates} & -\infty < z < \infty
\end{array}
$$
## integration element:
![[Pasted image 20251118210318.png]]
- **length element:** $\vec{dL} = d\rho\,\vec{a_\rho}\;+ \rho\,d\phi\,\vec{a_\theta}\;+ dz\,\vec{a_z}$
- **surface element:** $\vec{dS} = \rho\,d\phi\,dz\,\vec{a_\rho}+ d\rho\,dz \,\vec{a_\phi} +d\rho\,\rho\,d\phi\,\vec{a_z}$
	- $\vec{dS_\rho} =  \rho\,d\phi\,dz\,\vec{a_\rho}$
	- $\vec{dS_\phi} = d\rho\,dz \,\vec{a_\phi}$
	- $\vec{dS_z} = \rho\,d\phi\,\,d\rho\,\vec{a_z}$
- **volume element:** $dV = \rho\, d\rho \,d\phi\,dz$
## point transformation:
$$
\large\begin{array}{l}
(x,y,z) \Rightarrow (\rho,\phi,z)\quad: &
\rho = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\quad,\quad \phi = \tan^{-1} \frac{y}{x} \\
(\rho,\phi,z) \Rightarrow (x,y,z)\quad: &
x=\rho\cos\phi\quad,\quad y=\rho\sin\phi \\
\end{array}
$$
## vector transformation:
![[Pasted image 20251118203818.png]]
## cylindrical to cartesian:
$$
\begin{array}{c}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{a_\rho} \\
		\vec{a_\phi} \\
		\vec{a_z}
	\end{bmatrix}
		=
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\cos\phi & \sin\phi & 0 \\
		-\sin\phi & \cos\phi & 0 \\
		0 & 0 & 1
	\end{bmatrix}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{a_x} \\
		\vec{a_y} \\
		\vec{a_z}
	\end{bmatrix} 
	&\quad
	\begin{aligned}
		\vec{a_\rho} &= \cos\phi\,\vec{a_x} + \sin\phi\,\vec{a_y} \\
		\vec{a_\phi} &= -\sin\phi\,\vec{a_x} + \cos\phi\,\vec{a_y} \\
		\vec{a_z} &= \hat{a}_z
	\end{aligned}
\end{array}
$$
## cartesian to cylindrical:
$$
\begin{array}{c}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{a_x} \\
		\vec{a_y} \\
		\vec{a_z}
	\end{bmatrix}=
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\cos\phi & -\sin\phi & 0 \\
		\sin\phi & \cos\phi & 0 \\
		0 & 0 & 1
	\end{bmatrix}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{a_\rho} \\
		\vec{a_\phi} \\
		\vec{a_z}
	\end{bmatrix}
	\quad&
	\begin{aligned}
		\vec{a_x} &= \cos\phi\,\vec{a_\rho} - \sin\phi\,\vec{a_\phi} \\
		\vec{a_y} &= \sin\phi\,\vec{a_\rho} + \cos\phi\,\vec{a_\phi} \\
		\vec{a_z} &= \hat{a}_z
	\end{aligned}
\end{array}
$$
## generally:
$$
\left(
	\begin{array}{c | c c c}&
		\vec{a_\rho} & \vec{a_\phi} & \vec{a_z} \\
		\hline
		\vec{a_x} & \cos\phi  & -\sin\phi & 0 \\
		\vec{a_y} & \sin\phi  & \cos\phi  & 0 \\
		\vec{a_z} & 0 & 0 & 1
	\end{array}
\right)
$$
# Spherical coordinates ($r,\theta,\phi$):
$$
\begin{array}{l}
r : \text{starts from origin and toward +ve z-axis} & 0\le r\,<\infty \\
\theta : \text{starts from+ve z-axis toward -ve z-axis} & 0 \le \phi \le\;\pi \\
\phi : \text{like in cylindrical coordinates} & 0 \le \phi < 2\pi
\end{array}
$$
## integration elements:
![[Pasted image 20251027203135.png]]
- **length element:** $\vec{dL} = dr\,\vec{a_r}\;+r\,d\theta\,\vec{a_\theta}\;+r\sin\theta\,d\phi\,\vec{a_\phi}$
- **surface element:** $\vec{dS} = r^2\sin\theta\,d\theta\,d\phi\,\vec{a_r}+r\sin\theta\,dr\,d\phi\,\vec{a_\theta}+ r\,dr\,d\theta\vec{a_\phi}$
	- $\vec{dS_r} = r^2\sin\theta\,d\theta\,d\phi\,\vec{a_r}$
	- $\vec{dS_\theta} = r\sin\theta\,dr\,d\phi\,\vec{a_\theta}$
	- $\vec{dS_\phi} = r\,dr\,d\theta\vec{a_\phi}$
- **volume element:** $dV =r^2\sin\theta\,dr\,d\theta\,d\phi$
## point transformation:
$$
\large\begin{array}{l}
(x,y,z) \Rightarrow (r,\theta,\phi)\quad: &
r = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2} & \theta = \cos^{-1} \frac{z}{r} & \phi = \tan^{-1} \frac{y}{x} \\
(r,\theta,\phi) \Rightarrow (x,y,z)\quad: &
x=r\sin\theta\,\cos\phi& y=r\sin\theta\,\sin\phi& z = r\cos\theta \\
(r,\theta,\phi) \Rightarrow (\rho,\phi,z)\quad: & \rho = r\sin\theta & \phi=\phi &z=r\cos\theta
\end{array}
$$
## vector transformation:
![[Pasted image 20251118210645.png]]
## spherical to cartesian:
$$
\begin{array}{cc}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{a_r} \\
		\vec{a_\theta} \\
		\vec{a_\phi}
	\end{bmatrix}
	=
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\sin\theta\cos\phi & \sin\theta\sin\phi & \cos\theta \\
		\cos\theta\cos\phi & \cos\theta\sin\phi & -\sin\theta \\
		-\sin\phi & \cos\phi & 0
	\end{bmatrix}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{a_x} \\
		\vec{a_y} \\
		\vec{a_z}
	\end{bmatrix}
	&
	\qquad
	\begin{aligned}
		\vec{a_r} &= \sin\theta\cos\phi\,\vec{a_x} + \sin\theta\sin\phi\,\vec{a_y} + \cos\theta\,\vec{a_z}\\
		\vec{a_\theta} &= \cos\theta\cos\phi\,\vec{a_x} + \cos\theta\sin\phi\,\vec{a_y} - \sin\theta\,\vec{a_z}\\
		\vec{a_\phi} &= -\sin\phi\,\vec{a_x} + \cos\phi\,\vec{a_y}
	\end{aligned}
\end{array}
$$
## cartesian to spherical:
$$
\begin{array}{cc}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{a_x} \\
		\vec{a_y} \\
		\vec{a_z}
	\end{bmatrix}
	=
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\sin\theta\cos\phi & \cos\theta\cos\phi & -\sin\phi \\
		\sin\theta\sin\phi & \cos\theta\sin\phi & \cos\phi \\
		\cos\theta & -\sin\theta & 0
	\end{bmatrix}
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\vec{a_r} \\
		\vec{a_\theta} \\
		\vec{a_\phi}
	\end{bmatrix}
	&
	\qquad
	\begin{aligned}
		\vec{a_x} &= \sin\theta\cos\phi\,\vec{a_r} + \cos\theta\cos\phi\,\vec{a_\theta} - \sin\phi\,\vec{a_\phi}\\
		\vec{a_y} &= \sin\theta\sin\phi\,\vec{a_r} + \cos\theta\sin\phi\,\vec{a_\theta} + \cos\phi\,\vec{a_\phi}\\
		\vec{a_z} &= \cos\theta\,\vec{a_r} - \sin\theta\,\vec{a_\theta}
	\end{aligned}
\end{array}
$$
## generally:
$$
\left(
\begin{array}{c | c c c}
	 	     & \vec{a_r}    & \vec{a_\theta}         & \vec{a_\phi} \\
	\hline
	\vec{a_x} & \sin\theta\cos\phi & \cos\theta\cos\phi & -\sin\phi    \\
	\vec{a_y} & \sin\theta\sin\phi & \cos\theta\sin\phi & \cos\phi     \\
	\vec{a_z} & \cos\theta         & -\sin\theta        & 0
\end{array}
\right)
$$
