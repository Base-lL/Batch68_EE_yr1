# the work done on a moving point charge in an electric field
- suppose we wish to move a charge $Q$ a distance $dL$ in an electric field $E$ 
- the electric field exerts a force on the charge $F_E = QE$
- if we were to apply a force $F_\text{appl}$  on the charge, **the work done** by the force is: $W = \vec F \cdot \vec d = |F||d| \cos\theta$ 
- or $W = \int \vec F \cdot \vec{dL}$  where $dL$ is the component of the apply a force $F_\text{appl}$ in the direction of the electric field $E$
- work is measured in **joules ( J )** | **N.m**
## to move the charge from point *B* to point *A* :
$$
\large\begin{array}{l}
	dW = -Q\vec E \cdot \vec{dL}\\[3px]
	W= -Q \displaystyle\int_\text{initial}^\text{final} \vec E \cdot \vec {dL}\\[3px]
	W_{ab}= -Q \displaystyle\int_\text{b}^\text{a} \vec E \cdot \vec {dL}\\
\end{array}
$$

## work done due to point charge
assume a point charge $Q_1$ affected by another charge $Q_2$ and moving it from point A to B:
$$
\large\begin{align}
	W_{AB} &= -Q_1 \displaystyle \int_B^A \vec E \cdot\vec{DL} \\[3px]
	&=  -Q_1 \displaystyle \int^{r_A}_{r_B} \dfrac{Q_2}{4\pi\epsilon_0\,R^2} dr \\[3px]
	&= \dfrac{Q_1 Q_2}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \left[ \dfrac{1}{R} \right]_{\LARGE r_b}^{\LARGE r_a}\\[3px]
	W_{AB} &= \dfrac{Q_1 Q_2}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \left[ \dfrac{1}{r_A} - \dfrac{1}{r_B} \right]\\[3px]
	W_{AB} &= \dfrac{Q_1 Q_2}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \left[ \dfrac{1}{r_\text{ END}} - \dfrac{1}{r_\text{ START}} \right]\\
\end{align}
$$
where $r_A$ and $r_B$ are the distance better $Q_2$ **THE AFFECTING CHARGE** and the points A and B
## work done due to infinite line charge
assume a point charge $Q$ affected by a line charge $\rho_L$ and moving it from point A to B:
$$
\large\begin{align}
	W_{AB} &= -Q \displaystyle \int_A^B \vec E \cdot\vec{DL} \\[3px]
	&=  -Q \displaystyle \int_{ \rho_A}^{\rho_B} \dfrac{\rho_L}{2\pi\epsilon_0\,\rho} d\rho \\[3px]
	W_{AB} &= \dfrac{Q\,\rho_L}{2\pi\epsilon_0} \;\ln \left(\dfrac{\rho_A}{\rho_B} \right)\\[3px]
	W_{AB} &= \dfrac{Q\,\rho_L}{2\pi\epsilon_0} \;\ln \left(\dfrac{\rho_\text{ START}}{\rho_\text{ END}} \right)\\
\end{align}
$$
# potential difference "$V_{AB}$"
it is the work needed to move a positive charge from point B to A
$$
V_{AB} = V_A - V_B = \dfrac{W_{AB}}{Q} = -\displaystyle \int_B^A \vec E \cdot\vec{dL} \qquad:\text{ Volt | $\dfrac{\text{joule}}{\text{coulomb}}$}
$$

## potential difference due to point charge
$$
V_{AB} = \dfrac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \left[ \dfrac{1}{r_A} - \dfrac{1}{r_B} \right]\\[3px]
$$
## potential difference due to infinite line charge
$$
\large V_{AB} = \dfrac{\rho_L}{2\pi\epsilon_0} \;\ln \left(\dfrac{\rho_B}{\rho_A} \right)
$$
## remember A : start and B : end

# absolute potential "$V_A$"
- the potential at the point A with another reference point
- since potential difference at a point at infinity is zero, usually the reference point is $V_\infty$ , thus: $$\large V_{A} =-\int_\infty^A \vec E \cdot\vec{DL} = \dfrac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0\,r_A}$$
