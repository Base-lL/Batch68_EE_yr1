# in frequency domain
- frequency domain changes the circuit so that we can solve it like DC
- rules like current divider and voltage divider can be applied
- resistances can also be added in series or parallel
- *Note* the result will be a complex number

## Impedance ($Z$) :
$$
Z = R+j\left(\omega L -\dfrac{1}{\omega C} \right) = R+jX\qquad \text{, where: } X= X_L + X_C = \omega L -\dfrac{1}{\omega C}
$$
$$
Z = |Z|\angle\theta \quad,\quad \text{where: } Z = \sqrt{R^2 + X^2} \quad,\quad \theta = \tan^{-1}\left( \dfrac{X}{R} \right)
$$
$$
R = |Z|\cos\theta \quad,\quad X = |Z|\sin\theta
$$
## Admittance ($Y$) :
much like conductance, it is the inverse of impedance $Y = \dfrac{1}{Z}$ and it's units are in Siemens (S)
$$
V = IZ \quad\rightarrow\quad V = \dfrac{I}{Y} \quad\rightarrow\quad I = VY
$$
## note
$$
\text{if: }\quad Z=R+jX \quad,\quad Y = G+jB
$$
$$
\text{then: } G \ne \dfrac {1}{R} \quad,\quad B \ne \dfrac{1}{X} $$
$$
G = \dfrac{R}{R^2 +X^2} \quad,\quad B = \dfrac{-X}{R^2 +X^2}
$$
the components aren't just the inverse of one another, better to just use a calculator
## polar form
$$
Y = \dfrac{1}{Z} = \dfrac{ 1 \angle 0}{Z \angle \theta} = \dfrac{1}{Z}\angle-\theta
$$
notice that the angle is the same but with opposite signs
## Voltages
since complex numbers can represent rotations and $j$ is considered a $90\degree$ rotation:
- $V_R$ has no imaginary component so it stays in the real number line
- $V_L$ leads current by $90\degree$ so it is multiplied by $j$
- $V_C$ lags current by  $90\degree$ so it is multiplied by $-j$
$$
\begin{array}{l}
V_R = IR\\
V_L = I\cdot j\omega L  =  I\cdot j X_L\\
V_C = I\cdot \dfrac{-j}{\omega C} = -I\cdot j X_C\\
\end{array}
$$
## Voltage divider
$$
V_R = V \dfrac{R}{Z} \quad,\quad V_L = V \dfrac{j\omega L}{Z} \quad,\quad V_C = V \dfrac{\frac{j}{\omega C} }{Z}
$$
