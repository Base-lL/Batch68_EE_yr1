# Current and Current Density

$$
I = \iint \vec{J} \cdot d\vec{S} \quad [A]
$$


## For uniform cross section area:

$$
J = \frac{I}{A} \quad [A/m^2]
$$
---
# Convection Current Density

$$
J = \rho_v V \quad [A/m^2]
$$

* $\rho_v$ : Volume charge density $[C/m^3]$
* $V$ : Drift velocity $[m/s]$

---

# Point Form of Ohm's Law

$$
J = \sigma E \quad [A/m^2]
$$

$$
V_e = -\mu_e E \quad [m/s]
$$

## Electron Mobility ($\mu_e$):

$$
\mu_e = \frac{e T}{m}
$$

* $e$ : Electron charge $\approx -1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, C$
* $T$ : Average time between any two collisions
* $m$ : Electron mass $\approx 9.1 \times 10^{-31} \, kg$

---

# Conductivity ($\sigma$)

$$
\sigma = -\rho_e \mu_e = \frac{n e^2 T}{m} \quad [\text{Siemens}]
$$

where $\rho_e$ is the electron volume charge density $[C/m^3]$

# Continuity Equation

$$
\nabla \cdot \vec{J} = -\frac{\partial \rho_v}{\partial t}
$$

---

# Power Dissipated

$$
P = \iiint (\vec{E} \cdot \vec{J}) \, dv
$$

$$
P = V I = I^2 R
$$

$$
\vec{E} \cdot \vec{J} = \sigma |E|^2
$$

## Power density:

$$
w_p = \frac{dP}{dv} = \vec{E} \cdot \vec{J} = \sigma |E|^2
$$

---

# For any conductor with uniform cross section area:

$$
E = \frac{V}{L}
$$

## The Resistance:

$$
R = \frac{L}{\sigma A} = \frac{\rho L}{A}
$$

* resistivity: $\rho$  $= \dfrac{1}{\sigma} \quad [\Omega \cdot m]$

---

# For non uniform cross section:

$$
R = \frac{V}{I} = \frac{\int \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{L}}{\oint \vec{J} \cdot d\vec{S}}
$$
