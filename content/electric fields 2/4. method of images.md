for a medium that contains a set of charges whether it's *point*, *line* or *surface* charges and a **charged conductor surface**, we use this method

a conducting surface can be thought of as a mirror that reflects the charges above it
![[Pasted image 20260330111345.png]]
when a charge is reflected, it's sign gets inverted as shown

now you just have to deal with the set of charges only without the conducting surface



# Flat surfaces
the get the number of images formed:
$$
N = \frac{360}{\psi} - 1
$$
where $\psi$ is the angle between the conducting surfaces
*Note the value is 1 for an angle of 180 which is a completely flat surface*

to find the coordinates of the formed images quickly, use the polar coordinates $(r,\theta)$ and the following rule:
$$
x, y =r\cos(\phi_0 + n\cdot\psi) \;,\; r\sin(\phi_0 + n\cdot\psi)
$$
where $\phi_0$ is the starting angle of the first original charge
*NOTE*: if it says the charge lies between the 2 planes, $\phi_0 = \psi /2$

the rule is simple, you add the angle amount $\psi$ to the previous charge and the new charge's coordinate

it is better to be put inside a table like:
$$
\begin{array}{|c|c|}
\hline
Q & x & y & \vec R & |R| \\
\hline
Q_1 & r\cos(\phi_0) & r\sin(\phi_0) \\ 
Q_2 & r\cos(\phi_0 + \psi) & r\sin(\phi_0 + \psi) \\ 
Q_3 & r\cos(\phi_0 + 2\psi) & r\sin(\phi_0 + 2\psi)\\
\vdots &
\vdots &
\vdots &

\end{array}
$$




# sphere conductor and point charge
![[Pasted image 20260330214619.png]]

the same idea of transforming the conductor surface into a charge is applied here, with the mirror charge:
$$
Q_i = -\frac{a}{d}Q
$$
and it lies a distance from the center equal:
$$
d_i = \frac{a^2}{d}
$$
*(note: $Q_i$ and $d_i$ are the same as $Q'$ and $d'$ in the image, naming differences)*

for the case of an earthed conductor, these are all the rules
for non-earthed conductors with voltage $V$, it is considered as a charge in the center of the sphere with value:
$$
Q_c = 4\pi \epsilon_0 a V
$$
the surface charge density on a grounded sphere:
$$
\rho_s = D_n = \epsilon_0 E = \frac{Q}{4 \pi a}\left[ \frac{a^2 - d^2}{(a^2 + d^2 - 2ad\cos\theta)^{3/2} } \right]
$$

# Cylindrical conductor and line charge
similar to the sphere conductor in the formulas:

a line of charge density $\rho_i$ lies a distance $d_i$ away from the center of the cylinder where:
$$
 \rho_i = -\rho_L \quad,\quad d_i = \frac{a^2}{d}
$$
for a non earthed cylinder, it is substituted with:
$$
\rho_c = \dfrac{2\pi \epsilon_0 V}{\ln\frac{\rho_0}{a}}
$$
where $\rho_0$ is a reference where $V=0$ (*will be given*)
