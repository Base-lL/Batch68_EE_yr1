# Quick recap

$$
Q \xrightarrow{Q/S} \rho_s \xrightarrow{|D|=\rho_s} \vec{D} \xrightarrow{\vec{E}=\frac{\vec{D}}{\epsilon}} \vec{E} \xrightarrow{V=-\int \vec{E} \cdot dL} V \longrightarrow C=\frac{Q}{V}
$$

$$
V \xrightarrow{\vec{E}=-\nabla V} \vec{E} \xrightarrow{\vec{D}=\epsilon \vec{E}} \vec{D} \xrightarrow{\rho_s=|D|} \rho_s \xrightarrow{Q=\rho_s * S} Q \longrightarrow C=\frac{Q}{V}
$$
assuming $\epsilon_r$ is constant for the following cases

# energy stored

$$
W \;=\; \frac12 C V^2 \;=\; \frac12 QV \;=\; \frac12 \frac{Q^2}{C}
$$

# Two parallel plate

$$
C = \frac{\epsilon S}{d} \; , \quad \epsilon = \epsilon_r \epsilon_0
$$
the electric field in this case is uniform and is the same for any point between the two parallel plates
$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

## how to derive
assume that an electric charge $Q$ that is constant across the conductor surface
we then get the surface charge distribution $\rho_s = \frac{Q}{S}$

*from the recap section above:*
$$
\vec D = |\rho_s| \vec{a_z} = \frac{Q}{s}\vec{a_z}
$$

$$
\vec E = \frac{\vec D}{\epsilon} = \frac{Q}{\epsilon s}\vec{a_z}
$$

$$
V = -\int \vec E \cdot dL =\vec E \cdot -\int dL = E \cdot d = \frac{Qd}{\epsilon s}
$$

$$
C = \frac{Q}{V} = \frac{\epsilon s}{d}
$$

# Coaxial cable
$$
C = \frac{2\pi\epsilon L}{\ln(b/a)}
$$
- a is the inner radius of the cable
- b is the outer radius

## how to derive
assume that an electric charge $Q$ on the inner conductor

using gauss law:
$$
\oint_\text{surface} D\cdot\vec{dS} = Q_{en} \quad\rightarrow\quad \vec D \cdot (2\pi\rho L) = Q
$$

$$
\vec D = \frac{Q}{2\pi\rho L} \vec{a_\rho} \quad\Rightarrow\quad \vec E = \frac{Q}{2\pi\epsilon\rho L} \vec{a_\rho} 
$$

$$
\because V_{ab} = -\int^a_b \vec E \cdot \vec{dL} 
$$

$$
\therefore V_{ab} = -\int^a_b \frac{Q}{2\pi\epsilon\rho L}
\quad\Rightarrow\;
- \left[\frac{Q}{2\pi\epsilon L}\ln\rho\right]^a_b
$$

$$
V_{ab} = \frac{Q}{2\pi\epsilon L}\ln\frac{b}{a}
$$
we finally get
$$
C= \frac{2\pi\epsilon L}{\ln({b}/{a})}
$$






# concentrated spheres
$$
C = \frac{4\pi\epsilon}{[\frac{1}{a} - \frac{1}{b}]}
$$
- a is the inner sphere
- b is the outer sphere

# single sphere

$$
C = 4 \pi \epsilon a
$$
