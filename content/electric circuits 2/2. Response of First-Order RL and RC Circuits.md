remember the **inductor** and **capacitor** differential equations:
$$
L \frac{di}{dt} + Ri = 0 \quad,\quad C \frac{dv}{dt} + \frac{v}{R} = 0
$$

# The Natural Response
we have a circuit and switch that has been closed for a long time before opening at t = 0

long time refers to that all current and voltages reached *a constant value*

this means that in the components' differential equation, the derivative part is equal to zero


## The Natural Response of an RL Circuit

![[Pasted image 20260308234146.png]]


**before opening the circuit** $(t<0)$ , the inductor appears as a *short circuit*

**when the switch opens** $(t\ge0)$, all of the stored current inside the inductor gets released 
the inductor at this state can also be assumed as a *current source* that has a function with time $i(t)$ 
### Characteristics of the RL
the initial current is the same before and right after opening the switch
$$
i(0^-) = i(0^+) = I_0
$$
the current across the inductor at $t\ge0$ :
$$
i(t) = I_0 e^{-t/\tau} \quad,\quad \tau= \frac{L}{R}
$$
to get the work equation, substitute in:
$$
W(t) = \frac12  L\left[i(t)^2\right]
$$
to get the energy dissipated from the capacitor:
$$
W_d(t) = \frac12 L {I_0}^2 \left( 1 - e^{-2\,t/\tau} \right)
$$
to get the percentage of the dissipated energy:
$$
\text{dissipated energy \%}= 1 - e^{-2\,t/\tau}
$$

the maximum energy the capacitor can store (at $t \rightarrow \infty$) :
$$
W = \frac12 L {I_0}^2
$$

## The Natural Response of an RC Circuit

![[Pasted image 20260405153617.png]]

**before opening the circuit** $(t<0)$ , the capacitor appears as a *open circuit*

**when the switch opens** $(t\ge0)$, all of the stored current inside the capacitor gets released 
the capacitor at this state can also be assumed as a *voltage source* that has a function with time $v(t)$ 

### Characteristics of the RC
the initial voltage is the same before and right after opening the switch
$$
v(0^-) = v(0^+) = V_0
$$
the voltage across the capacitor at $t\ge0$ :
$$
v(t) = V_0 e^{-t/\tau} \quad,\quad \tau= RC
$$

to get the work equation, substitute in:
$$
W(t) = \frac12  C\left[v(t)^2\right]
$$
to get the energy dissipated from the capacitor:
$$
W_d(t) = \frac12 C {V_0}^2 \left( 1 - e^{-2\,t/\tau} \right)
$$
to get the percentage of the dissipated energy: *(same as the inductor)*
$$
\text{dissipated energy \%}= 1 - e^{-2\,t/\tau}
$$

the maximum energy the capacitor can store (at $t \rightarrow \infty$) :
$$
W = \frac12 C {V_0}^2
$$

# The Step response
step response refers to the component's behavior when a power source is suddenly applied
a switch is left open and is closed at t = 0
## step response of RL

![[Pasted image 20260405203722.png]]

assuming the inductor stored current $I_0$:
$$
i(t) = \frac{V_s}{R} + \left(I_0 - \frac{V_s}{R} \right) e^{-t/\tau}
$$
at $I_0 = 0$:
$$
i(t) = \frac{V_s}{R} \left( 1-e^{-t/\tau} \right)
$$
voltage across the inductor:
$$
v = V_s e^{-t/\tau}
$$


## step response of RC
![[Pasted image 20260405205128.png]]

assuming the inductor stored voltage $V_0$:
$$
v(t) = I_s R + \left(\;V_0 - I_s R \;\right) e^{-t/\tau}
$$
at $V_0 = 0$:
$$
i(t) = I_s R \left( 1-e^{-t/\tau} \right)
$$
current across the capacitor:
$$
i = \left( I_s - \frac{V_0}{R} \right)e^{-t/\tau}
$$

# General solution for first order circuits

$$
x(t) = x_f + \left[ x(t_o) - x_f \right] e^{(t_0 - t)/\tau}
$$
- $x(t)$ : the unknown variable as a function of time
- $x_f$ : the final value of the variable
- $x(t_0)$ : the initial value of the variable
- $t_0$ : the time of switching *(most of the time is zero)*



## general first steps
1. Identify the variable of interest for the circuit. For RC circuits, it is most convenient to choose the capacitive voltage; for RL circuits, it is best to choose the inductive current.
2. Determine the initial value of the variable, which is its value at t0.
3. Calculate the final value of the variable, which is its value as t : q .
4. Calculate the time constant for the circuit.
