# 2.1 Thermal motion
$\because$ Average electron/hole kinetic energy = $\frac{3}{2}  kT = \frac{1}{2} m v_{th}^2$
$$
\therefore V_{th} = \sqrt{\frac{3kT}{m_{eff}}} \;,\quad \text{at }T=300k:\quad V_{th} = 2.3\times10^7 cm/s
$$
## characteristics
- moves in zig-zag motion due to collisions or scattering with imperfections in the crystal.
- Net thermal velocity is zero. *(net displacement is zero)*

# 2.2 Drift motion
Drift is the motion of charge carriers caused by an **electric field**

## 2.2.1 Electron and Hole Mobility

$$
\begin{array}{l}
v = \mu_p E &,\quad -v = \mu_n E \\[4px]
\mu_p = \dfrac{q\tau_{mp}}{m_p} &,\quad \mu_n = \dfrac{q\tau_{mn}}{m_n}
\end{array}
$$
- $v$ : drift velocity
- $\mu_p / \mu_n$ : hole / electron mobility
- $m_p/m_n$ : hole / electron mass
- $\tau_m$ : mean average time between collisions

### Mean free path
$$
\text{Mean free path} = \tau_{mh} \times v
$$
this one wasn't mentioned directly in the lecture, rather found by using the MKS unit system: $L = S \times L\,S^{-1}$  

## 2.2.2 Mechanisms of Carrier Scattering
there are 2 main causes of carrier scattering:
### phonon scattering

The mobility due phonon scattering $\mu_\text{phonon}$ *increases with* the **mean free time** of phonon scattering (proportional)
$$
\mu_\text{phonon} \varpropto \tau_\text{phonon}
$$
and the mean free time $\tau_\text{phonon}$ *decreases* when we *increase* **phonon density** and **carrier thermal velocity** (inversely proportional)
$$
\tau_\text{phonon} \varpropto \frac{1}{\text{phonon density $\times$ carrier thermal velocity}}
$$
and both of these *increase* with **temperature**
$$
\tau_\text{phonon} \varpropto \frac{1}{T \times T^{1/2}} \varpropto \frac{1}{T^{3/2}} 
$$


### Ionized-Impurity (Coulombic) Scattering

$$
\mu_{impurity} \propto \frac{v_{th}^3}{N_a + N_d} \propto \frac{T^{3/2}}{N_a + N_d}
$$

### Total mobility

$$
\frac{1}{\tau} = \frac{1}{\tau_{phonon}} + \frac{1}{\tau_{impurity}}
$$

$$
\frac{1}{\mu} = \frac{1}{\mu_{phonon}} + \frac{1}{\mu_{impurity}}
$$
note that the **mean free time** is inverted because we are adding the scattering rates not the times itself

## 2.2.3 Drift Current and Conductivity

hole current density:
$$\because J = qpv \quad,\quad v = \mu_p E$$

$$
\therefore J_p = q p\,\mu_p E \quad,\quad J_n = q n\,\mu_n E
$$
for the total drift current
$$
J_\text{drift} = \sigma E = \left( q p\,\mu_p + q n\,\mu_n \right)E
$$
so the conductivity of a material is:
$$
\sigma = q p\,\mu_p + q n\,\mu_n 
$$

# 2.3 Diffusion Current

Generally, Particles diffuse from a higher-concentration location
to a lower-concentration location.

![[Pasted image 20260409212917.png]]

If these particles have charge, this would indicate charge
crossing a certain place (charge transfer means current)

diffusion current is given by:
$$
J_{n,\text{diff}} = qD_n \frac{dn}{dx} \quad,\quad J_{p,\text{diff}} = -qD_p \frac{dp}{dx}
$$
where $D$ is called the diffusion constant

the reason diffusion current for holes is negative is because they walk in the direction different to the electrons
![[Pasted image 20260409213654.png]]

# Total current

$$
J_\text{total} = J_n + J_p
$$
where:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
J_n = J_{n,\text{drift}} + J_{n,\text{diff}} =qn\mu_n E + qD_n \frac{dn}{dx}\\[2.5px]
J_p = J_{p,\text{drift}} + J_{p,\text{diff}} =qp\mu_p E - qD_p \frac{dp}{dx}
\end{array}
$$

# 2.5 Einstein Relationship
a relationship between the diffusion constant $D$ and mobility $\mu$

for a piece of non-uniformly doped semiconductor, if *and only if* it is at  **equilibrium** and so there is no current $J_n$ or $J_p$ :
$$
\begin{array}{l}
D_n = \frac{kT}{q} \mu_n \quad,\quad D_p = \frac{kT}{q} \mu_p
\end{array}
$$

# 2.6 Electron-Hole Recombination
$$
\begin{array}{l}
n = n_0 + n' \\
p = p_0 + p'
\end{array}
$$
where:
- $n_0 / p_0$ are **equilibrium** carrier concentrations
- $n' / p'$ are the **excess** carrier concentrations

where the **equilibrium** carriers are the $n/p$ carriers we were dealing with earlier.

notice that the total electrons/holes can be different from $n_0 / p_0$ because of the added **excess** carriers

this happens when the material is not at *equilibrium* that is, it is being affected by an external energy source

## Charge Neutrality

at *equilibrium* i.e the material is at rest: $n' = p' = 0$
when light is shined on the material, it is no longer at equilibrium but **Charge Neutrality** states that if a non-zero $n'$ is present, an equal $p'$  to maintain charge equality (and vice-versa): $n' = p'$

## Recombination Lifetime

assuming light generates $n'$ and $p'$ 
if light is *suddenly* turned off, both of them will decay over time until they become zero
this process is called **recombination**
The time constant of decay is the **recombination time** or **carrier lifetime** ($\tau$) which ranges from $1\,ns$ to $1\,ms$ and is typically around $1\,\mu s$ *(in silicon)* and depends on the density of metal impurities

### Rate of recombination

$$
\frac{dn'}{dt} \quad=\; -\frac{n'}{\tau} \;=\; -\frac{p'}{\tau} \;= \quad\frac{dp'}{dt}
$$

# 2.8 Quasi-equilibrium and Quasi-Fermi Levels

for the 2 equations:
$$
n = N_c\,e^{ -\left( E_c - E_f \right)/kT } \quad,\quad p = N_v\,e^{ -\left( E_f - E_v \right)/kT }
$$
they are only valid at equilibrium where $n' = p' = 0$ and $np = {n_i}^2$

in the cases that $n' = p' \ne 0$ and $np \ne {n_i}^2$, to keep these relations, we introduce 2 **Quasi-fermi levels** $E_{fn}$ and $E_{fp}$ where:
$$
n = N_c\,e^{ -\left( E_c - E_{fn} \right)/kT } \quad,\quad p = N_v\,e^{ -\left( E_{fp} - E_v \right)/kT }
$$

at equilibrium, $E_{fn} = E_{fp} = E_f$
